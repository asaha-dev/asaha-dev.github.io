evaluation - measuring progress vs decision support#evals #leadership
Evaluation often drives some of the most consequential choices teams make. Should we ship? Should we delay? Should we stop investing in a direction altogether? At its core, evaluation exists to support these decisions. Yet when you ask a model or system development team how they evaluate their work, the conversation usually begins with metrics. Teams can often describe a broad collection of benchmarks, scores and performance indicators. What is less common is a clear articulation of how those metrics connect to the decisions being made. Which metric would actually cause a launch to be delayed? Which benchmark would justify additional investment? Which result would make us reconsider the direction entirely? Without that connection, evaluation can become an exercise in measuring progress rather than supporting decisions.read more →
leadership lessons#leadership
Leadership styles are often described as fixed traits. My experience has been almost the opposite. The instincts that made me effective at one stage of my career have often required recalibration as the organization changed, the work changed, and the people around me changed. For a long time, my default style was hands-on coaching. I stayed close to the work, shaped direction through direct input, and often built alongside the team.
